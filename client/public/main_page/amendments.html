Amendments to Part 3: Fundamental Rights

The amendments to Part 3 of the Constitution have introduced several key changes over time. These amendments include modifications to the scope of fundamental rights, the introduction of new rights, and adjustments to existing provisions. For example:

  • Amendment X: Expanded the scope of the right to equality.
  • Amendment Y: Added new provisions related to freedom of speech and expression.
  • Amendment Z: Clarified the protection against discrimination on the grounds of gender.

Amendments to Part 4: Directive Principles of State Policy

The Directive Principles of State Policy have been periodically amended to reflect the changing socio-economic conditions and policy priorities. Key amendments include:

  • Amendment A: Enhanced the focus on environmental protection.
  • Amendment B: Emphasized the promotion of education and healthcare.
  • Amendment C: Updated provisions related to social justice and economic welfare.

Amendments to Part 4A: Fundamental Duties

Amendments to Part 4A have focused on strengthening the obligations of citizens towards their country. Key changes include:

  • Amendment 1: Expanded the list of fundamental duties to include environmental responsibilities.
  • Amendment 2: Included duties related to national integrity and harmony.
