Part 3: Fundamental Rights

Part 3 of the Constitution guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens. These rights include freedom of speech, right to equality, and protection against discrimination. It ensures that every citizen has the right to a fair trial, freedom of assembly, and freedom of religion.

Part 4: Directive Principles of State Policy

Part 4 outlines the directive principles that guide the state in making policies and laws. These principles aim to ensure social and economic justice, promote welfare, and uphold the dignity of the individual.

Part 4A: Fundamental Duties

Part 4A emphasizes the duties of citizens to uphold the sovereignty, integrity, and unity of the nation. It includes duties such as respecting the Constitution, following laws, and promoting harmony among all citizens.

Preamble

The Preamble serves as an introductory statement that outlines the objectives of the Constitution. It declares the intention to establish a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic and to secure justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity for all citizens.
